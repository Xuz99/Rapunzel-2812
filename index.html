Chapter I Act I

In the future, the world is terminally ill with cancer known as the human race. The population is crushed under the grinder that is turned by the rich and powerful. The environment ruined in ecocide with soil that is toxic and barren and water poisoned and deserted of life. Forced by the hand of fate, the human race have no choice but to exist in squalid mega-block buildings that pack hundreds of thousands into one building, living and dying under one roof, all suffocating in the consequences of their ancestors.

This story follows the affairs of a beggared mining family that live in desperation to begin their own family. The couple slaved over agonising shifts at the mine and feared they would never produce offspring. Until a speck of luck fell upon them, the woman became with a child.

Chapter II

The family dwelled behind a pharmacy, which was owned by a wealthy man known as Eugene Solomon, in amidst the slums. Guarded by high walls and the latest in thief deterrent equipment, only a fool dared to trespass.

As time had passed the rarity of medicine in the world had increased drastically, it had become extortionate to buy and so needed to be well protected.

Chapter III

One day the man was gazing outside as the pharmacy was receiving a shipment of medication under armed guard. He took note that they had received a shipment of Rapunzel anti-virus serum, as they delicately transported the small set of vials inside.

In desperate need of the serum, as waves of the rampant infections, viruses, and diseases plagued the slum, jeopardising his future child`s survival, he prayed to vaccinate his wife to provide a future for their child.

Chapter IV

As her pregnancy advanced, her health deteriorated. Her body growing weak and frail. Her husband distraught watching his wife die, grew desperate and knew that the serum would save her life. So he quickly formulated a plan to try and break into the pharmacy for his family.

Chapter V

Using his skills as a miner, he attempts to break in by digging a tunnel under the pharmacy's wall, only to trip the alarm and be apprehended immediately.

Chapter VI

The pharmacy owner heard the alarm of the break in and proceeded to viciously attack the husband.

However, before the husband is beaten to death he pleads with Solomon to spare his life so he can save his wife. The husband explains his cause, their child, his wife and the future of their family rests on the cure, to which the pharmacy owner agrees to let him live as well as let him take as much of the serum as he needs.

The only condition being when the child is born the pharmacy owner will take the child as his own, away from the family. The husband, so fearful for his life and the life of his wife, agrees.

Chapter VII

The woman makes an extraordinary recovery and produces a baby girl.

Once the child´s cries are heard, the pharmacy owner arrived and forcefully removed the child from the family.

Chapter VIII

The child is clear of all deformities and ailments because of the serum, as such the pharmacy owner names the child Rapunzel. As she grows older she remains physically healthy, with nothing wrong with the now young woman who grew to be extremely beautiful with gorgeous, ever growing blonde hair.

Chapter IX

With of the rarity of a human being born without deformity or ailment, the pharmacy owner decided to lock the girl away in a floating tower high above the pharmacy. The only access to the tower being a small port for air cars to land on.

Chapter X

However, the security system on the ports door meant that a specific passphrase needed to be said anytime the owner wished entry into the tower. This phrase was...

Chapter XI

"Rapunzel, Rapunzel,
Let down your hair!”

Chapter XII

When such a pass code was said, the port doors would open and then an enormous light bridge would extend from within the tower, which allowed the owner to walk in.

Chapter XIII Act II

One day, Rufus Colt, a rebel leader of the resistance heard rumours of a beautiful, untouched tower high in the sky above a pharmacy in the slums. He believed it would be a profitable raid target.

Chapter XV

Once he gained entry to the tower, he was stunned by the beautiful light bridge. However, he found nothing of serious value.

No huge stores of food, just enough food for one person to live off, and medicine was the same. Only enough for one person. No weapons, money, technology or hardware to sell on or steal for his own gain. As he kept on searching it was becoming more and more apparent that there was nothing of worthwhile here, so why would a rich pharmacy owner have nothing of value in such a heavily fortified and secured tower.

Travelling along the brightly lit corridor to the library, his ears filled with delight, a voice so melodic like he was hearing an angel sing.

Chapter XVI

As he walked on, she appeared dominating his view. Rapunzel. Rufus was completely stopped in his tracks he had no idea how to react to someone so beautiful. She was the idea of perfection. Her hair was as if it was spun as fine gold by the angels of god. Her eyes were two beautifully lit crystals on her pale face that looked as if they held the Milky Way galaxy flowing within them. Her physique was well balanced not overweight like an opera singer or too underweight like the under feed children in the slums. She wore the finest of velvet dresses coloured deep shades of brown, with a finely crafted leather corset on the outside of the dress.

But it had to be her smile he was mesmerised by the most. It was so bright and childlike. Every second he stared at her smile it was as if her soul was glowing out of her smile and the light it shined out for her was covering him in a feeling of happiness and love he never knew he could feel.

Chapter XVII

After a few moments of them both staring at each other, she realized that there was someone else in the world besides her captor.

At first, she was frightened by the rebel leader, but at the same time she was also excited she had a new person in her life.

After the first encounter, they became friends very quickly and agreed to meet every day. Then after a while, they both fell in love with each other and decided that she should escape from the tower as soon as they could get out.

Chapter XVIII

Solomon did not discover the relationship until one day when he arrived early and found the rebel leader still in the tower. Outraged, he tried to kill Rufus. Rapunzel pulled them from each other and told her captor that killing the rebel leader would kill the father of her child. She could not bear for that to happen so she pleaded with the pharmacy owner. Instead of killing the rebel leader, he decided he would never be allowed to see the women he loved ever again...

Chapter XIX

So he blinded Rufus by taking his eyesight, and then banished them both into the slums.

Chapter XX Act III

Rapunzel was left to wander the slums, heavily pregnant with twins, and stripped of all the luxuries she was used to in the tower.

Rufus was kicked out of the tower and left for dead in the streets below. He managed to survive, but he desperately longed for the women he loved.

For over a year, he tried to look for her, but without eyesight he couldn’t find her. No description seemed to fit anyone he asked about her. He assumed the worst and was beginning to believe she was long dead along with any chance of their children being alive.

Chapter XXI

Then as he was walking through the slums one day, his heart caught aflame again. The voice. It rung in his ears and trembled down to his bones.

He immediately went into a frenzy, trying to search for the source. As he got closer, the voice continued to get louder, until he found a small run down slum house with the sound of two children playing outside.

Again, he could not see, so he was relying entirely on his hearing. But he was sure this voice was the same majestic voice of Rapunzel.

As he opened the door, she turned around ready to defend herself from an intruder, but instantly she recognised the man she loved.

Chapter XXII

They both embraced each other as she cried with tears of joy. He fell to his knees, as he couldn’t believe he had found her again after so long.

Their eyes meet, his still blinded. Her tears fell from her face onto his ruined eyes, and slowly his vision began to recover.

Chapter XXIII

The first thing he is able to see is that same smile that blossomed off her and covered him in love. He looked at her and saw an angel-like glow surrounding her as they both fell madly in love all over again.

Chapter XXIV

He took her and the children back to the rebel headquarters, where the family could be safe from harm and learn to fight for a better life in this dystopian hell. From that day on, they lived happily ever after.


The End
